# Important Topics for ECT402 - Wireless Communication

- **Generations of Wireless Communication Systems:** The evolution, features, and performance comparisons of 1G (analog), 2G, 3G, 4G, and 5G technologies.
- **Wireless LANs (WLAN) and PANs:** The architecture and components of WLANs, the differences between Infrastructure and Ad-hoc (peer-to-peer) network topologies, and Bluetooth network structures (Piconets and Scatternets).
- **Cellular Concept and System Design:** The fundamentals of cellular architecture, frequency reuse, and frequency reuse distance calculations.
- **Channel Assignment Strategies:** The concepts of Fixed Channel Allocation (FCA), Dynamic Channel Allocation (DCA), and Hybrid Channel Allocation.
- **Handoff Strategies:** The mechanisms of transferring active calls between cells, including hard handoff, soft handoff, improper vs. proper handoff scenarios, and the factors influencing handoff decisions.
- **Improving Coverage and Capacity:** Techniques used to allow more channels per unit coverage area when cells become congested, primarily focusing on **cell splitting**, **cell sectoring**, **microcell zone concepts**, and **repeaters**.
- **Interference and System Capacity:** Understanding co-channel interference, adjacent channel interference, trunking, and Grade of Service (GoS)
