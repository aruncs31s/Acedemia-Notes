# 2.3 Capacity of Wireless Channels

## Overview

> **Capacity** = Maximum data rate (bits/sec) at which information can be transmitted with arbitrarily small error probability.

Wireless channels are **fading channels** — signal strength varies due to multipath propagation. Unlike wired channels, capacity is not fixed but depends on channel state.

---

## 2.3.1 Review of Capacity in AWGN

### Shannon Capacity Formula

$$
C = B \log_2(1 + \text{SNR})
$$

**Where:**
- $B$ = Bandwidth (Hz)
- $\text{SNR}$ = Signal-to-Noise Ratio (linear, not dB)

**Key Insight:** In AWGN, capacity increases logarithmically with SNR. Doubling bandwidth doubles capacity.

### Important Relationships

| Parameter | Formula |
|-----------|---------|
| SNR | $P_r / N_0 B$ |
| Noise Power | $N_0 B$ ($N_0$ = noise spectral density) |
| Channel Capacity | $C = B \log_2(1 + P_r / N_0 B)$ |

---

## 2.3.2 Capacity of Flat Fading Channel – Ergodic Capacity

### What is Ergodic Capacity?

When the channel fades (varies over time), we consider the **average capacity** over all possible channel states. This is **ergodic capacity**:

$$
\bar{C}_{\text{ergodic}} = \mathbb{E}_h \{ B \log_2(1 + \rho |h|^2) \}
$$

**Where:**
- $h$ = channel gain (random variable)
- $\rho$ = average SNR
- $\mathbb{E}_h\{\}$ = expectation over channel distribution

### Key Points

1. **Ergodic capacity** = long-term average capacity across fading states
2. Depends on the **probability distribution** of the channel gain $|h|^2$
3. For **Rayleigh fading**: need to integrate over Rayleigh distribution
4. This gives the **average** data rate achievable over fading channel

### Interpretation

- If the channel is good most of the time, capacity is high
- Deep fades (poor channel states) reduce average capacity
- **Key takeaway**: Fading channels have lower capacity than AWGN with same average SNR

### Without CSI (Channel State Information)

- Transmitter does not know the channel
- Uses **constant power** and **fixed rate**
- Capacity = Ergodic capacity (average over fading)

## 2.3.3 Capacity with Outage

### What is Outage?

**Outage** = When the instantaneous data rate falls below a target rate, causing transmission failure.

### Outage Capacity

The capacity such that the probability of outage is below a threshold:

$$
P_{\text{out}} = P\{C < R_{\text{target}}\}
$$

### Key Concept

| Metric | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Outage Capacity $C_{\text{out}}$ | Achieved with specified outage probability $q$ |
| $q$-Outage Capacity | Data rate guaranteed $(1-q)\%$ of time |

### Formula

For a target rate $R$, the outage probability is:

$$
P_{\text{out}} = P\{ \log_2(1 + \rho |h|^2) < R \}
$$

### Practical Use

- Used when **constant data rate** is required
- Trade-off: Higher target rate → higher outage probability
- System designed for $1\%$ outage → works $99\%$ of time

### Comparison

| Approach | Description | When Used |
|----------|-------------|-----------|
| Ergodic Capacity | Average rate | Delay-tolerant apps |
| Outage Capacity | Guaranteed rate with $q\%$ outage | Real-time apps (voice/video) |

## 2.3.4 Capacity with CSI (Channel State Information)

### What is CSI?

**CSI** = Knowledge of channel gain $h$ at the transmitter and/or receiver.

### Types of CSI

| Type | Full Form | Description |
|------|----------|-------------|
| CSIR | CSI at Receiver | Receiver knows channel; transmitter does not |
| CSIT | CSI at Transmitter | Transmitter knows channel |
| CSIT+CSIR | Full CSI | Both ends know channel |

### With CSIR (Receiver knows channel)

- **Still uses constant transmit power**
- Receiver can **adapt decoding** based on channel state
- **No capacity gain** over ergodic — still need averaging

### With CSIT (Transmitter knows channel)

**KEY: Capacity can be significantly increased!**

The transmitter can **adapt its power and rate** based on channel state:

#### Water-Filling Algorithm

When transmitter knows $h$:
$$
P(h) = \left( \mu - \frac{1}{|h|^2} \right)^+
$$

**Water-filling:** More power when channel is strong, less (or zero) when weak.

### Key Results

| CSI Type | Capacity | Adaptive? |
|----------|----------|-----------|
| No CSI | Ergodic capacity | No |
| CSIR | Same as ergodic | No adaptation |
| CSIT + Rate | Increases | Rate adaptation |
| CSIT + Power + Rate | Maximum | Full adaptation |

### With Full CSI (CSIT + CSIR)

- **Transmitter adapts:** power $P(h)$ and rate $R(h)$
- **Optimal strategy:** Water-filling over channel states
- **Result:** Near-optimal to AWGN capacity for each channel state

### Practical Insight

**With CSI at transmitter:**
- Good channel → High rate, high power
- Poor channel → Low rate, low power (or wait)
- This achieves **near-optimal capacity**

## Summary Table

| Channel Type | Capacity Formula | Key Insight |
|--------------|------------------|-------------|
| AWGN | $B \log_2(1 + \text{SNR})$ | Fixed channel |
| Fading (No CSI) | $E_h[B \log_2(1 + \rho |h|^2)]$ | Ergodic = average |
| Fading (CSI-R) | Same as no CSI | No gain from CSIR alone |
| Fading (CSI-T) | Water-filling | Adaptive power |
| Fading (Full CSI) | Max of water-filling | Maximum capacity |

---

## Key Formulas to Remember

1. **AWGN Capacity:** $C = B \log_2(1 + \text{SNR})$
2. **Ergodic Capacity:** $\bar{C} = E_h\{B \log_2(1 + \rho |h|^2)\}$
3. **Outage Probability:** $P_{\text{out}} = P\{C < R_{\text{target}}\}$
4. **Water-filling:** $P(h) = (\mu - 1/|h|^2)^+$

---

## Important Concepts

- ✓ Capacity in wireless depends on **channel distribution** (fading)
- ✓ **Ergodic capacity** = average over all fading states
- ✓ **Outage capacity** = guaranteed rate with specified outage probability
- ✓ **CSI at transmitter** enables adaptation → significant capacity gain
- ✓ **Water-filling** = optimal power allocation with CSI
- ✓ Derivations NOT required — only expressions and significance
